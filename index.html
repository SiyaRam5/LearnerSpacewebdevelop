How to Get Started

Want to start playing basketball? Here's what you need to know:

  • Equipment: You'll need a basketball and a hoop. Standard hoops are 10 feet high.
  • Basic Rules:
    • Basketball is played with two teams of five players each.
    • The objective is to score points by shooting the ball through the opponent's hoop.
    • You can move the ball by dribbling (bouncing the ball) or passing it to a teammate.
    • Each team has a basket that they defend while trying to score in the opponent's basket.
    • A game typically consists of four quarters, each lasting 10-12 minutes.
  • Positions: Players usually have specific roles such as:
    • Point Guard: The team's primary ball handler and playmaker.
    • Shooting Guard: A player who is good at shooting from long distances.
    • Small Forward: A versatile player who can play both offense and defense.
    • Power Forward: A strong player who plays near the basket.
    • Center: The tallest player who plays near the basket and is responsible for rebounding and shot-blocking.
  • Basic Techniques:
    • Dribbling: Use your fingertips to bounce the ball on the floor. Keep your head up to see the court.
    • Shooting: Aim for the square on the backboard for layups, and use proper form for jump shots.
    • Passing: Use chest passes, bounce passes, and overhead passes to move the ball to teammates.
    • Defense: Stay between your opponent and the basket. Use your body and hands to block shots and passes.
  • Game Play: The game starts with a jump ball at center court. Teams score points by making field goals (2 or 3 points) or free throws (1 point).
  • Practice: To improve, practice dribbling, shooting, passing, and defensive skills regularly. Work on conditioning to stay fit and agile.
